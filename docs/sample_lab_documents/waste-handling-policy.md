# Lab Waste Handling and Safety Policy

This is the active waste handling policy for our wet-lab space. It covers
biohazard, chemical, and sharps waste plus emergency response. All lab members
must read this policy on day one and re-acknowledge it annually. Owner:
Daniel Okafor, Lab Safety Officer (okafor@example.lab).

## Required training before any hands-on work

Before touching any reagent or biological sample you must complete:

- The institution-wide "Lab Safety Foundations" online course (about 90
  minutes)
- The bloodborne pathogen module if you handle human or primate samples
- A 30-minute walk-through of waste stations, eyewash, safety shower, and
  first-aid kit locations with Daniel Okafor
- Sign the safety acknowledgement form (PDF on the lab Notion under
  "Onboarding / Forms")

PPE expectations: lab coat, closed-toe shoes, and nitrile gloves at all
times in the wet bench area. Safety glasses are required when handling any
chemical or open-tube biological work.

## Biohazard waste

Biohazard waste goes in the orange-bagged autoclave bins located beneath
each bench. Do not overfill above the marked line. Bags are autoclaved by
the central facility every Tuesday and Thursday morning; place full bags
in the pickup bin in the hallway outside B-210 by 9 AM.

Sharps go in the rigid red sharps containers, never in autoclave bags.
Replace a sharps container when it is two-thirds full.

## Chemical waste

Each bench has a labeled satellite accumulation container for organic
solvents. Aqueous chemical waste goes in the blue-capped jugs near the
fume hood. Do not mix waste streams — when in doubt, ask Daniel Okafor
or the bench owner before pouring.

Pickup is monthly via Environmental Health and Safety. Daniel submits the
pickup ticket on the last Friday of each month; tag any container that
needs immediate pickup with the orange "FULL" sticker.

## Spill response

For chemical spills smaller than 100 mL of a non-volatile reagent, use
the spill kit under the fume hood. For anything larger, more volatile,
or unknown:

1. Evacuate the immediate area.
2. Call EHS at extension 4-7777.
3. Notify Daniel Okafor by phone, then Slack.

The eyewash station is at the corner of the wet bench by the door. The
safety shower is in the hallway, four steps left of the lab entrance.
Both are tested monthly; if they fail, do not use the bench area until
they are restored.

## Incident reporting

Any spill, exposure, near-miss, or injury must be reported within 24
hours via the EHS web form (link in the lab Notion under "Safety"). Even
near-misses are useful — they help us catch failure points before they
become incidents.
