# Getting Started — Your First Week

Welcome. This is the unofficial-but-official orientation for new lab members.
The goal is to get you oriented, safe, and connected to the right people in
the first five days. Owner: Sara Kowalski (kowalski@example.lab).

## Day 1 — Orientation

- Arrive at 9 AM. Sara Kowalski will meet you in the lobby for a tour.
- Sign the safety acknowledgement form before going into the wet bench
  area.
- Get your lab door PIN from Sara.
- Read the waste handling policy and the data storage conventions
  documents end to end. Both are in the lab Notion under "Onboarding".
- Set up Slack and join: `#lab-general`, `#imaging`, `#lab-data`,
  `#tissue-culture`, plus any project-specific channels your PI flags.

By end of day you should be able to find the safety shower, eyewash
station, fire extinguisher, and waste stations without help.

## Day 2 — Safety training

- Complete the institution's "Lab Safety Foundations" online course.
- Schedule the in-person safety walk-through with Daniel Okafor (30
  minutes). This is required before any hands-on work — without it you
  cannot get fume hood or tissue culture access.
- If you're working with biological samples, complete the bloodborne
  pathogen module.

## Day 3 — Equipment introductions

- Meet with Priya Mehta for an imaging tour, even if your project doesn't
  involve microscopy yet — most projects eventually do.
- Meet with Marcus Cho if your project touches cell culture.
- Meet with Yuki Tanaka to review the equipment booking calendars and
  ordering process.
- Read the Microscope SOP if you'll be doing any imaging.

## Day 4 — Access verification

- Use the access checklist to confirm every account and door is working.
- Mount `/lab-storage` and verify you can read and write to the right
  folders for your project.
- Log into Benchling and find your project workspace. Create your first
  notebook entry — even just a "starting on day 4" stub.

## Day 5 — Shadow and plan

- Shadow at least one full workflow run with a trained lab member. Watch
  the whole thing end to end including cleanup. Don't drive yet.
- Sit down with your project lead and write a one-page plan for your
  first two weeks of independent work.
- Add yourself to the lab roster document with your contact info and
  what you'll be working on.

## Common first-week pitfalls

- Skipping the safety walk-through and getting blocked on hood access at
  end of week one.
- Storing data on a personal laptop instead of `/lab-storage`. Move it
  before someone notices.
- Booking a microscope slot before completing the supervised session.
  Priya will not sign off on your first solo run if you skipped the
  walk-through.
- Not joining the lab Slack channels. Most lab coordination happens
  there, not over email.

## Who to ask when you're stuck

- Process or "where does this go" questions: Sara Kowalski.
- Safety: Daniel Okafor.
- Equipment-specific: see the lab roster's Equipment ownership table.
- Science direction: your project lead, then Dr. Vasquez.
- Anything urgent: post in `#lab-general` and someone will route it.

You're not expected to know any of this on day one. Ask early and often
— that's how the lab stays welcoming.
